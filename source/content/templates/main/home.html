We follow the Japanese Philosophy called "Kaizen" Underlying the Kaizen approach is a dedication to improving effectiveness, satisfaction, and waste. The core tenets of continuous improvement of Kaizen include:
1. Standardizing a process so that it’s repeatable and organized
2. Focusing on measurability and evaluating progress using data
3. Comparing results against your requirements (did you deliver on your promise?)
4. Innovating new and better ways to achieve similar results
5. Responding to changing circumstance and evolving your methods over time

Sample Question

You are given an m x n integer grid accounts where accounts[i][j] is the amount of money the i​​​​​​​​​​​th​​​​ customer has in the j​​​​​​​​​​​th​​​​ bank. Return the wealth that the richest customer has. A customer's wealth is the amount of money they have in all their bank accounts. The richest customer is the customer that has the maximum wealth.

Example 1:

Input: accounts = [[1,2,3],[3,2,1]]

Output: 6

Explanation: 1st customer has wealth = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 2nd customer has wealth = 3 + 2 + 1 = 6 Both customers are considered the richest with a wealth of 6 each, so return 6.

Example 2:

Input: accounts = [[1,5],[7,3],[3,5]]

Output: 10

Explanation: 1st customer has wealth = 6 2nd customer has wealth = 10 3rd customer has wealth = 8 The 2nd customer is the richest with a wealth of 10.

Example 3:

Input: accounts = [[2,8,7],[7,1,3],[1,9,5]]

Output: 17
Constraints: m == accounts.length n == accounts[i].length 1 <= m, n <= 50 1 <= accounts[i][j] <= 100
